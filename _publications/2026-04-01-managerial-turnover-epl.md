excerpt: 'This paper studies the effect of managerial replacements in the English Premier League using a stacked event study design that exploits quasi-random variation in dismissal timing across 83 trajectory-matched events (1992-2025). We find that short-run performance gains reflect a normalization of finishing luck rather than genuine improvement in chance creation, and that performance gains depend on clubs committing to a permanent replacement.'
